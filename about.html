How It Began

The Green Classroom grew out of fieldwork in agriculture and nutrition projects, where agricultural information in Pakistan was found to be scattered, overly technical, and rarely framed through the lens of political economy.

The Green Classroom was created to change that. By consolidating dispersed knowledge and presenting it in an accessible, politically informed way, the project provides researchers with an easier starting point that highlights farming practices, power relations, and stakeholders shaping Pakistanâ€™s agricultural economy.

The Challenge

Pakistan's agricultural sector contributes significantly to the national economy, yet lacks comprehensive mapping of its supply chains. This gap creates inefficiencies, information asymmetries, and barriers to sustainable development.

Our research team identified the urgent need for a systematic approach to document, analyze, and visualize these complex networks that connect millions of farmers to consumers across the country.

Our aim is to connect researchers to the agricultural sector through the information we have collected from farmers, end buyers, transporters, retailers, and wholesalers â€” building a bridge between knowledge and practice.
