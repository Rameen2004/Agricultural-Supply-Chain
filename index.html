Supply Chain Map

1
⚠
Raw material
Choosing high-quality rice seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides
Raw Material Sourcing Details
Rice varieties are carefully selected based on regional climate conditions and market demand. Premium seeds undergo rigorous quality testing with germination rates exceeding 95%. Organic fertilizers are increasingly preferred, comprising 35% of total fertilizer usage.
Seed Selection Time: 4-6 weeks
Quality Testing: 12-point verification system
Top Varieties: Basmati, Jasmine, Arborio
Critical Issues: Seed quality inconsistency, climate change impact, varying pesticide regulations
Raw Material Bottlenecks
Seed quality inconsistency, climate change impact, varying pesticide regulations
2
⚠
Rice cultivation
Land preparation, plantation, optimal temperature, water, and sunlight
Rice Cultivation Details
Modern rice cultivation employs precision agriculture techniques with GPS-guided equipment. Water management systems reduce consumption by 30% compared to traditional methods. Temperature monitoring ensures optimal growing conditions between 20-35°C.
Growing Period: 100-150 days
Water Usage: 3,000-5,000 liters per kg
Yield Potential: 4-6 tons per hectare
Critical Issues: Water scarcity, labor shortages, extreme weather events
Rice Cultivation Bottlenecks
Water scarcity, labor shortages, extreme weather events
3
⚠
Hulling/Husking
Removal of outer husk, can be used for construction material and as fertilizers
Hulling/Husking Details
Modern hulling facilities process up to 10 tons per hour with 98% efficiency. Husks are increasingly used for sustainable packaging, biofuel production, and organic fertilizers. Automated optical sorting removes 99.5% of impurities during this stage.
Processing Speed: 5-10 tons/hour
Husk Byproduct: 20% of total weight
Energy Consumption: 45 kWh per ton
Critical Issues: Equipment maintenance downtime, inconsistent grain size, rising energy costs
Hulling/Husking Bottlenecks
Equipment maintenance downtime, inconsistent grain size, rising energy costs
4
⚠
Drying
To reduce moisture content. 45°C to avoid cracking grains.
Drying Process Details
Controlled drying reduces moisture content from 25% to 14% for optimal storage. Solar drying technologies are increasingly adopted, reducing energy costs by 40%. Temperature monitoring systems prevent overheating and maintain grain quality.
Optimal Moisture: 12-14%
Drying Time: 8-24 hours
Quality Retention: 95% with controlled drying
Critical Issues: Inconsistent drying leading to mold, rising energy costs, climate variability
Drying Bottlenecks
Inconsistent drying leading to mold, rising energy costs, climate variability
5
⚠
Threshing
Rice grains are separated from the stalks
Threshing Process Details
Modern threshing machines achieve 99% grain recovery with minimal damage. Computerized systems adjust threshing speed based on stalk moisture content. Straw byproducts are increasingly collected for biomass energy production.
Processing Rate: 1-3 tons/hour
Grain Loss: Less than 1% with modern equipment
Straw-to-Grain Ratio: 1.5:1
Critical Issues: Grain breakage, equipment availability limitations, rising maintenance costs
Threshing Bottlenecks
Grain breakage, equipment availability limitations, rising maintenance costs
6
⚠
Harvesting
Can be manual or mechanized
Harvesting Details
Mechanized harvesting has increased from 35% to 65% in the last decade. GPS-guided harvesters reduce waste by 12% compared to manual methods. Optimal harvest timing is determined using satellite imagery and moisture sensors.
Harvesting Window: 7-10 days
Labor Requirement: 25 person-hours per hectare (manual)
Mechanized Efficiency: 2-3 hectares per hour
Critical Issues: Labor shortages, weather disruptions, equipment breakdowns
Harvesting Bottlenecks
Labor shortages, weather disruptions, equipment breakdowns
7
⚠
Milling
Polishing, colour sorting, and grading. Broken rice used for animal feed or rice flour
Milling Process Details
Advanced optical sorting technology removes 99.9% of discolored grains. Milling degree is customized based on market preferences, with 5-7% bran removal for brown rice and 8-10% for white rice. Broken rice is repurposed for value-added products.
Milling Recovery: 65-70%
Processing Speed: 3-8 tons/hour
Broken Rice Yield: 10-15% of total
Critical Issues: Power fluctuations, equipment calibration issues, skilled operator shortages
Milling Bottlenecks
Power fluctuations, equipment calibration issues, skilled operator shortages
8
⚠
Packaging
Package the product
Packaging Details
Modern packaging uses modified atmosphere technology to extend shelf life by 40%. Sustainable packaging options have increased by 65% in the last five years. QR codes enable full traceability from farm to consumer.
Package Sizes: 1kg, 5kg, 10kg, 25kg
Shelf Life: 12-24 months
Packaging Materials: HDPE, paper, biodegradable films
Critical Issues: Material shortages, equipment malfunctions, higher costs for sustainable options
Packaging Bottlenecks
Material shortages, equipment malfunctions, higher costs for sustainable options
9
⚠
Transportation
Send it for transportation
Transportation Details
GPS tracking systems monitor 85% of rice shipments in real-time. Temperature-controlled containers maintain optimal conditions throughout transit. Blockchain technology is increasingly used for shipment verification and documentation.
Average Distance: 1,500-3,000 km
Transport Modes: Truck (65%), Rail (20%), Ship (15%)
Transit Time: 3-21 days depending on destination
Critical Issues: Fuel price volatility, port congestion, cross-border documentation issues
Transportation Bottlenecks
Fuel price volatility, port congestion, cross-border documentation issues
10
⚠
Wholesaler
Storage in warehouse
Wholesaler Operations Details
Modern warehouses utilize IoT sensors to monitor temperature, humidity, and pest activity. Inventory management systems optimize stock rotation with FIFO principles. Direct-to-retailer distribution models have reduced handling costs by 18%.
Average Storage Time: 2-6 months
Optimal Storage Conditions: 15-18°C, 60-65% humidity
Inventory Turnover: 6-8 times annually
Critical Issues: Market price volatility, warehouse capacity constraints, pest management challenges
Wholesaler Bottlenecks
Market price volatility, warehouse capacity constraints, pest management challenges
